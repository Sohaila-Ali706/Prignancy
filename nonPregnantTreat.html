Non Pregnant / Treatment

non pregnant:

Interestingly, marital status plays a role. Research indicates that married women have a higher prevalence of incontinence (42%) compared to their single counterparts (25.7%). Why Does This Happen?
Several factors contribute to SUI like:
• Events like childbirth can weaken the muscles supporting the bladder.
• Hormonal Fluctuations especially during menopause, decreased estrogen levels can affect urinary control.

Management

Preformed at home or physiotherapy center

Note: Initial assessment preferably done by a PT to ensure proper technique.
Duration : 10-15 minutes daily .
Reps: 3 sets of 15-20 contractions per session .
Instructions: Focus on performing long holds (10-15 seconds) for each contraction. Increase intensity progressively.

PT Center only

Duration: 10-20 minutes per session
Reps: Dependent on your progress
Instructions: Use external sensors (e.g., vaginal or anal sensors) to provide feedback during pelvic floor contraction and relaxation exercises.

PT Center only

Duration: 20 minutes per session, 2-3 times a week
Reps: As per treatment plan.
Instructions: Use electromagnetic stimulation to activate pelvic floor muscles, combined with visceral manipulation techniques to enhance pelvic support.
