Virgin / Treatment

Virgin:

While SUI is often associated with factors like childbirth, studies have shown that it can also occur in women who haven't given birth. Research indicates that approximately 29% of nulliparous (women who have never given birth) experience some form of urinary incontinence, with stress incontinence being a common type.

Why Does This Happen?
Several factors can contribute to SUI in virgins:
• High-impact sports or exercises can put pressure on the bladder, leading to leaks.
• Extra weight can increase abdominal pressure, affecting bladder control.
Smoking has been linked to a higher risk of urinary incontinence.

Instructions: Use external sensors (e.g., surface electrodes) to provide real-time feedback during pelvic floor contractions.
