Pregnant / Prevention

FIRST TRIMESTER:

This is when you’re settling into pregnancy, and your body is adjusting. Gentle pelvic floor exercises, like Kegels, can help build strength in your pelvic muscles, which supports your bladder. You can start doing these in short sessions throughout the day.

Management

Description: Understanding the importance of pelvic floor muscles during pregnancy and learning basic contraction techniques.
Duration & Repetitions : One educational session; practice contractions of the pelvic floor muscles (as if stopping the flow of urine)by holding for 3-5 seconds, relaxing for 3-5 seconds, 10 repetitions, twice daily.
Setting: Physiotherapy center for initial education; subsequent exercises can be performed at home.

Description: Gentle Kegel exercises focusing on contracting the pelvic floor muscles (as if stopping the flow of urine)and relaxing these muscles.
Duration & Repetitions: Hold contraction for 3 seconds, relax for 3 seconds; 10 repetitions, twice daily.
Setting: Initially taught at a physiotherapy center; then continued at home.

SECOND TRIMESTER

As your belly grows, your pelvic floor needs extra support. Keep up with your pelvic floor exercises and add some stretching to avoid discomfort. Stay hydrated and take breaks when standing to help prevent pressure on the bladder.

Management

Description: Strengthening pelvic floor muscles through consistent Kegel exercises.
Duration & Repetitions: Hold contraction of the pelvic floor muscles (as if stopping the flow of urine) 5 seconds, relax for 5 seconds; 10 repetitions, three times daily.
Setting: Can be performed at home; periodic check-ins at a physiotherapy center recommended.

Description: Using RTUS to visualize and correct posture, ensuring proper alignment and reducing strain.
Duration & Repetitions: One session per week for 4 weeks.
Setting: Physiotherapy center.

Third TRIMESTER

In the final stretch, your little one’s weight can increase the pressure on your bladder. Maintain those Kegels, and be mindful of your posture! Also, wearing breathable, comfy clothes helps reduce pressure and lets you stay active and mobile.

Management

Description: Coordinating deep breathing with pelvic floor contractions to enhance muscle control.
Duration & Repetitions: Inhale deeply, contract pelvic floor muscles during exhale, hold for 3 seconds; 10 repetitions, twice daily.
Setting: Home.

Description: Engaging in specific postures to reduce intra-abdominal pressure and activate pelvic floor muscles.
Duration & Repetitions: Hold each posture for 10 seconds; 5 repetitions, once daily.
Setting: Initially under supervision at a physiotherapy center; then at home.
