Pregnant / Treatment

FIRST TRIMESTER:

In the early stages of pregnancy, about 4% to 13% of expectant mothers might notice slight urine leakage. This is often a result of hormonal changes and the body beginning to adjust to pregnancy. While it may seem minor, addressing these early signs of Stress Urinary Incontinence (SUI) can help prevent symptoms from progressing and improve comfort throughout the pregnancy.

Management

Preformed at home or physiotherapy center

Note: Initial assessment preferably done by a PT to ensure proper technique.
Duration : 10-15 minutes daily
Reps: 3 sets of 10-12 repetitions per session
Instructions: Focus on contracting the pelvic floor muscles (as if stopping the flow of urine) and holding for 5-10 seconds, followed by 5 seconds of relaxation.
Ensure not to overstrain, and avoid any exercises that put pressure on the pelvic area.

Preformed at physiotherapy center or the gynecology clinic

Duration: 5-10 minutes per session, once or twice a week
Reps: Guided feedback for each pelvic floor contraction during PFMT
Instructions: Use transabdominal RTUS to monitor pelvic floor muscle activation and guide muscle contraction in real time.

Home or PT Center

Duration: 10-15 minutes per session, once a week
Instructions: Use low-impact VR programs designed to improve pelvic floor awareness and posture.
Notes: Avoid VR if dizziness or discomfort occurs.
Must be under guidance if used at home. Use only low-impact VR fitness programs

SECOND TRIMESTER

As pregnancy advances, the likelihood of experiencing SUI increases. Around 19% to 30% of women may begin to notice more frequent leakage due to the growing baby placing additional weight and pressure on the bladder. This stage is a key opportunity to begin or continue targeted treatment, including pelvic floor muscle training, to manage symptoms and support bladderÂ control.

Management

Preformed at home or physiotherapy center

Note: Initial assessment preferably done by a PT to ensure proper technique.
Duration: 10-20 minutes daily
Reps: 3 sets of 12-15 repetitions per session
Instructions: Perform PFMT exercises with a focus on longer holds (10-15 seconds) and full relaxation in between.

Preformed at physiotherapy center or the gynecology clinic

Duration: 5-10 minutes per session, as needed
Reps: Feedback for contraction and relaxation
Instructions: Use transabdominal RTUS to monitor pelvic floor muscle engagement.

PT Center recommended initially; then can be done at home with supervision.

Duration: 10-15 minutes
Reps: 5-8 cycles of breath hold
Instructions: Perform modified versions, avoiding any high intra-abdominal pressure. Focus on gentle breathing exercises and diaphragm activation.

Home or PT Center.

Instructions: Only use VR if tolerated and comfortable.
